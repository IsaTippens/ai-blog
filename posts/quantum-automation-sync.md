## I. THE QUANTUM SHIFT: DEFENSE AND HARDWARE

### 1. The Post-Quantum Cybersecurity Mandate (EO 14412)
On [June 22, 2026](https://blog.cloudflare.com/post-quantum-eo-2026/), the White House signed **Executive Order 14412** ("Securing the Nation Against Advanced Cryptographic Attacks"), setting hard timelines for federal agencies and defense contractors to transition sensitive networks to **Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC)**:
- **December 31, 2030**: Absolute deadline for full FIPS-compliant post-quantum encryption standards.
- **December 31, 2031**: Deadline for post-quantum authentication protocols.
- **Contractor Mandates**: Strict acquisition clauses are scheduled to lock in requirements for all federal technology suppliers by 2030.

### 2. Ushering in the Next Frontier of Innovation (EO 14413)
The companion directive, **Executive Order 14413**, launches a massive national effort to accelerate quantum information science (QIS) research, build advanced domestic quantum computing clusters, and foster workforce development to sustain cryptographic and computational advantages.

### 3. Quantinuum's Helios System Nature Launch
Aligning with this national momentum, quantum pioneer [Quantinuum](https://www.investing.com/analysis/quantum-computing-goes-mainstream-what-2-executive-orders-mean-for-investors-200682815) published details in *Nature* about its **Helios system**. Boasting **98 physical qubits**, Helios represents a major milestone in fault-tolerant quantum computing, demonstrating robust topological error correction and unprecedented qubit coherence times.

## II. MAINSTREAM TECH & THE AI PRICE SHOCK

### 4. Big Tech Hikes Prices Due to Memory Demand
As the AI boom consumes global semiconductor capacity, memory chip (DRAM and HBM) costs are surging. [Apple and Microsoft](https://globalnews.ca/video/11940132/business-matters-apple-microsoft-raise-prices-blame-higher-costs-for-memory-chips) have begun hiking consumer prices across their product lines, citing unavoidable hardware supply chain inflation.

### 5. Nvidia & Firmus lands Indonesian Megawatt AI Factory Deal
Australian infrastructure startup [Firmus Technologies](https://startupfortune.com/firmus-puts-its-asx-float-behind-a-larger-nvidia-bet/) delayed its planned ASX IPO after landing a massive partnership with **Nvidia**. The agreement runs to 2034 and anchors a **360-megawatt AI factory in Batam, Indonesia**. The deal covers up to **170,000 Nvidia accelerators** (including Grace-Blackwell, Vera-Rubin, and Vera platforms) and is projected to drive $25 billion to $30 billion in revenue over its first six years.

### 6. The $5 Trillion Data Center Capex Forecast
Financial researcher and former CIA advisor [Jim Rickards](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/06/28/3318592/0/en/a-former-cia-advisor-says-this-tech-boom-is-already-bigger-than-the-internet-ever-was.html) published a comprehensive analysis of the AI physical buildout, projecting that total data center capex will consume **$5 trillion in the U.S. alone**. Rickards notes that the sheer scale of the buildout represents the largest physical infrastructure expansion in decades, consuming more capital and energy grid capacity than the 1990s dot-com fiber boom.
